## üìú Legal Framework References

### Primary Ethiopian Laws Integrated:

#### 1. **Criminal Code of Ethiopia (Proclamation No. 414/2004)**
The app references these key articles:

- **Article 553** - Harassment and Stalking
  - Covers persistent unwanted contact and cyberstalking
  - Penalties: Fine or imprisonment

- **Article 564** - Intimidation and Threat
  - Covers threats of violence or harm
  - Penalties: Up to 3 years imprisonment

- **Article 589** - Defamation
  - Covers false statements damaging reputation
  - Penalties: Fine or imprisonment

- **Article 625** - Sexual Harassment
  - Covers unwanted sexual advances and explicit content
  - Penalties: Imprisonment

- **Article 640** - Sexual Offenses
  - Covers non-consensual intimate image sharing
  - Penalties: Significant imprisonment

- **Articles 459-461** - Perjury
  - Referenced in affidavit attestation section
  - Warns against false statements

#### 2. **Computer Crime Proclamation (No. 958/2016)**
Modern cybercrime legislation:

- **Article 5** - Illegal Access and Interception
  - Unauthorized access to computer systems/data
  
- **Article 6** - Data Interference
  - Unauthorized deletion, alteration, or distribution

- **Article 8** - Computer-Related Fraud and Identity Theft
  - Fake profiles, impersonation, identity fraud
  - Penalties: Imprisonment and fines

#### 3. **Telecom Fraud Offences Proclamation (No. 761/2012)**
- Covers telecommunications-based harassment
- Relevant for phone/SMS harassment

#### 4. **Ethiopian Civil Code (1960)**
- **Book V** - Obligations arising from Torts and Unjust Enrichment
  - Civil remedies for damages
  - Compensation for harm
  - Injunctive relief

#### 5. **FDRE Constitution (1995)**
- **Article 26** - Right to Privacy
  - Protection from unlawful interference
  - Basis for doxxing and privacy violation cases
